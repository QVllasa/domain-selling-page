## Design Context

### Users
Premium domain buyers — investors, startup founders, and brand managers looking for short, memorable .io domains. They browse on desktop and mobile, often evaluating multiple domains. They expect a high-end, trustworthy experience that signals the domain's value.

### Brand Personality
**Refined, Exclusive, Confident.** The interface should feel like walking into a private gallery — minimal distractions, every element intentional, quiet authority. No flashiness, no desperation. The domain sells itself.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Dark luxury — deep noir backgrounds, warm gold accents, generous whitespace
- **Theme**: Dark mode (correct for premium/luxury context — evening browsing, considered purchase)
- **References**: High-end auction houses, luxury watch brand sites, private equity landing pages
- **Anti-references**: GoDaddy, cheap domain marketplaces, SaaS landing pages with gradient CTAs
- **Palette**: Noir (#07070A) base, cream (#EEEEE8) text, gold (#C8A84E) accent — 60/30/10 rule

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet confidence** — Let whitespace and typography do the work. No screaming, no clutter.
2. **Every pixel earns its place** — If an element doesn't serve the sale, remove it.
3. **Tactile refinement** — Interactions should feel like handling a well-made object: smooth, weighted, precise.
4. **Typography as architecture** — Type hierarchy creates the spatial structure, not decorative elements.
5. **Restraint over spectacle** — One perfect animation beats ten mediocre ones.
